I am an applied economist working on how production risks, sustainability challenges, and policy environments shape outcomes in agricultural and food systems. My research integrates economic modeling with biological and production data to analyze market adjustment, supply-chain resilience, and long-term productivity. I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher at Kansas State University. My recent work focuses on evaluating the economic implications of alternative production and risk-management strategies in livestock systems, with broader interest in sustainability transitions across agricultural sectors. With training in applied statistics and experience using data-driven modeling approaches, I collaborate in interdisciplinary research environments to generate insights relevant for producers, industry stakeholders, and policy discussions.
